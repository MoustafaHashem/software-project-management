\section{Business Case}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|l|X|}
\hline
\textbf{The Problem} & Universities require reliable course registration systems to manage large numbers of students registering concurrently while enforcing academic rules. Manual or poorly designed systems often result in prerequisite violations, timetable conflicts, and unfair seat allocation. \\
\hline
\textbf{The Opportunity} & Automation can significantly improve efficiency, accuracy, and student satisfaction \\
\hline
\textbf{Purpose} & The purpose of this project is to design and implement a scalable online course registration portal that enforces registration rules automatically, handles concurrent access correctly, and ensures data consistency under high load. \\
\hline
\textbf{Strategic Context} & Academic institutions need scalable, reliable systems to handle growing student enrollment and complex registration rules while maintaining data integrity under concurrent access \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\section{Project Description}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|X|}
\hline
The Online Course Registration Portal is a web-based system that automates the course registration process for university students. The system provides a user-friendly interface where students can browse available courses, check prerequisites, register for courses, and manage their academic schedules. \par
\vspace{6pt}
The portal enforces all academic policies automatically, including prerequisite validation, credit limit enforcement, and timetable conflict detection. It manages course capacity efficiently through a waitlist system that automatically allocates seats when they become available. The system is designed to handle high concurrent access during peak registration periods while maintaining data consistency and reliability. \par
\vspace{6pt}
This project delivers both the technical infrastructure and the functional capabilities required to support modern academic registration workflows in a distributed, scalable environment. \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\section{High-Level Risks and Assumptions}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|l|X|}
\hline
\multicolumn{2}{|c|}{\textbf{Key Risks}} \\
\hline
\multicolumn{2}{|X|}{
- Integration issues between distributed components \par
- Race conditions during concurrent registration causing data inconsistencies \par
- Complexity of prerequisite and conflict rules leading to implementation challenges \par
- Performance bottlenecks under high user load \par
- Time limitations affecting testing depth and quality assurance \par
- Scope creep from stakeholder requests \par
- Key resource unavailability
} \\
\hline
\multicolumn{2}{|c|}{\textbf{Assumptions}} \\
\hline
\multicolumn{2}{|X|}{
- Team members are available throughout the semester \par
- Required tools and frameworks are accessible \par
- Student and course data are available or can be simulated \par
- Team members have sufficient technical background \par
- System will be deployed in a controlled academic environment \par
- Subject matter experts will be available for consultation \par
- Necessary computing resources will be provided
} \\
